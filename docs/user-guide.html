User & Administrator Guide

How every module works, how to use it, and how the modules connect to each other — written from a direct audit of the running application, not the sales deck.

01 — Orientation

What NexusLine is

NexusLine is a one-stop Governance, Risk & Compliance platform built for the depth Pakistani banks need — SBP ETGRM, AML/CFT, Basel operational risk, Shariah governance, PDPA — while covering the breadth of a mature GRC suite: risk, compliance, policy, audit, continuity, third-party oversight and more. It ships as multi-tenant SaaS or as an on-premise install for banks that require data sovereignty.

Every bank that uses the platform is a fully isolated tenant — see Shared concepts, §3.

02 — Orientation

Getting started

Signing in

The login screen asks for three things: your Organization (your bank's short slug, e.g. acme), Email, and Password. Bookmark the login URL once you know your organization's slug.

  • If your account has MFA enabled, you'll be asked for a 6-digit authenticator code after your password.
  • After 5 failed attempts your account locks for 15 minutes (both figures are admin-configurable).
  • Sessions expire after 60 minutes by default — sign in again after that.
  • If your bank has SSO configured, a "Sign in with SSO" button redirects to your identity provider instead of a local password.
  • If your bank has LDAP / Active Directory configured, your normal directory password is checked instead of, or alongside, a local one.
ℹ
Directory- and SSO-managed accounts can't change their password inside NexusLine — that happens at the source (AD / your identity provider).

How you get an account

There is no self-signup. An account is created one of two ways: an administrator creates you directly under Users & Roles, or — if your bank has LDAP/SSO with just-in-time provisioning enabled — your account is created automatically the first time you sign in through the directory or identity provider.

Bootstrap checklist for a new bank deployment

  1. Org and first Admin user are created by the platform operator.
  2. Admin signs in and configures SSO or LDAP (on Settings) if the bank uses one — otherwise use local accounts.
  3. Create the compliance/risk/IT team under Users & Roles, or let LDAP JIT provisioning populate them.
  4. Set up Business Units (your branch/division hierarchy) and Processes.
  5. Install the standards you need from the Framework Library — ISO 27001, NIST CSF, SBP ETGRM, PCI DSS, Basel, Shariah.
  6. Build the Control Catalog, mapping to the requirements the installed frameworks created.
  7. Populate IT Assets and Information Assets and link them so criticality inherits correctly.
  8. Set your organization's risk appetite/tolerance, then start the Risk Register.
  9. Onboard Vendors, layering Outsourcing records for SBP-material or cloud arrangements.
  10. Turn on whichever specialist modules your bank needs — AML, Fraud, ICFR, BIA, Shariah, Whistleblowing, Declarations, and so on.
03 — Orientation

Concepts that apply to every module

Learn these once — they show up almost everywhere and the rest of this guide won't repeat them per module.

3.1 — The generic workflow status

On top of its own business status, nearly every record also carries a second, lightweight "is this record itself finalized" tracker:

draft→in_review→approved→retired

3.2 — RecordPanels, the shared toolkit on every record

Open almost any record — risks, controls, vendors, incidents, policies, BIA, continuity, ICFR, issues, model risk, projects, Shariah, vulnerabilities, whistleblowing, assets, and more (roughly 30 modules) — and you'll find the same three panels:

  • Custom Fields — any extra fields your admin defined for that record type (§11.2) appear automatically.
  • Review & Attestation — a periodic sign-off tracker: set a frequency, click "Attest now," see who confirmed the record and when it's next due. This is where most "management sign-off" requirements are actually satisfied, even on modules with no dedicated approval button.
  • Collaboration — comments, tags, and file attachments on any record that has this panel. This, not the dedicated Evidence module, is the general-purpose "attach a document" mechanism.

3.3 — Multi-tenancy & data isolation

Each bank's data is walled off from every other bank at the database level — even a buggy query cannot leak across tenants, and if the tenant tag is ever missing the system shows zero rows rather than everything. Your users, roles, SSO/LDAP configuration, custom fields, filters, and dashboards are all private to your organization.

⚠
Business Units are a label, not a security boundary. Records can be organized by branch or division for reporting, but nothing stops a user with a module's read permission from seeing every business unit's records in it — only whole-organization isolation is enforced.

3.4 — Evidence vs. attachments

Evidence (§7) specifically proves a Control is operating — every evidence item must point at a control. Collect it once per control and it counts toward every Compliance requirement that control maps to. Attachments on any other record type go through the Collaboration panel above, not the Evidence module.

3.5 & 3.6 — Reference numbers, and shared services

Most modules auto-generate a reference number when you save (e.g. REG-001, VLN-014) — you never type these. Import/export, custom fields, and status rules are System-section services that plug into modules rather than modules of their own — see §11.

Dashboard

/dashboard

Read-only executive snapshot of live risk, compliance, and control posture — the default landing page.

Governance Health

A composite 0–100 score, banded Healthy / Elevated / Critical, blended from the share of risks within tolerance, overall compliance %, and the residual critical/high risk mix.

Needs your attention

An auto-generated, prioritized queue — tolerance breaches, critical inherent risks pending treatment, frameworks below 80% compliant, overdue asset reviews, pending risk acceptances — each row with a one-click action button (Escalate / Assign / Review / Approve) linking straight into the relevant module.

Metric strip

Total risks, Critical inherent, Overall compliant %, Annual exposure (ALE), Tolerance breaches.

Risk matrix & compliance

A bubble chart (Inherent/Residual toggle, bubble size = risk count per cell) plus appetite/tolerance scores, and compliance donut rings — one overall, one per framework.

Roll-up & activity

An enterprise risk roll-up table by category, and a recent-activity feed filterable to 30 days / Quarter / YTD.

Key action

"Executive summary" PDF export.

Note

Content is still fixed to risk/compliance metrics — for a customizable dashboard, use Reports & KPIs instead.

How to use it

⚠

Strategy & Goals

/goals

Track strategic goals with a recurring pass/fail audit cycle.

How to use it

Create a goal (name, owner, related Risks / Projects / Policies) → set an audit frequency and success criteria → periodically Record audit (pass/fail + auditor + conclusion), which auto-reschedules the next audit date.

Status flow
not_started→on_track→at_risk→off_track→achieved
Connects to

Many-to-many with Risks, Projects, Policies. CSV import/export supported.

AI Assist

/ai-assistCircular Intelligence

A text-extraction workspace — paste text, get a structured extraction.

How to use it

Paste text (no PDF upload) → choose a source type (circular / policy / free text / incident) → choose an extraction type: Extract obligations, Summarize, Suggest risks, or Map to ISO 27001 controls → click Run extraction. Results save with a reference number in a searchable history; Copy pastes the output into the module it belongs to.

Offline vs. online

No API key configured → a deterministic offline heuristic runs, clearly labeled, no data leaves your deployment. A key configured → calls the AI model (labeled in the result) and silently falls back to the heuristic on any failure — a request never crashes.

⚠
No natural-language "ask a question about my data" chat exists — only these four fixed modes. Results are not auto-filed into other modules; you copy them over yourself.
Sidebar section

Risk

The risk register itself, plus the specialist registers that feed it — operational risk, scenario & capital, model risk, assets, and third parties.

Risk Register

/risks

The platform's central qualitative (5×5) and quantitative (FAIR) risk log — the hub most other modules link back to.

How to use it

Create a risk → General (title, category, owner) → Assessment (inherent likelihood/impact 1–5, plus optional FAIR fields: annual loss frequency, single loss expectancy → auto-computed ALE) → Links & Relations (Assets, Controls, Threats, Vulnerabilities, Policies, Incidents) → Review (frequency). Set your organization's Appetite/Tolerance thresholds once, from the settings panel on this page — every risk is then banded against it.

Status flow
draft→assessed→treatment_planned→treatment_in_progress→accepted→closed
Key actions
  • Appetite / Tolerance editor
  • Import / Export CSV
  • "Register PDF" export
Connects to

Assets, Controls, Threats, Vulnerabilities, Policies, Incidents, Goals, Vendors, and optionally a Risk Quantification record.

Operational Risk

/operational-risk

Basel-style RCSA, Key Risk Indicators, and the operational loss-event database.

RCSA tab

Create an assessment campaign (business unit, process, assessor, period), then add risk/control lines inside it — inherent/residual scoring, control effectiveness, remediation action + owner.

Key Risk Indicators tab

Define an indicator (unit, frequency, direction, warning/limit thresholds), then log measurements over time. Current RAG status (green/amber/red) is computed automatically from the latest value vs. thresholds.

Loss Database tab

Log a loss event — Basel event type, business line, gross loss, recovery, dates, root cause.

Status flows
planned→in_progress→completed
open→under_investigation→recovered→closed
ℹ
Shares the 7 Basel event-type taxonomy with Scenario & Capital's calculator, below.

Scenario & Capital

/scenario-analysis

Forward-looking op-risk scenario workshops, plus Basel III SMA operational-risk capital calculation.

Scenario Library

Record a workshop — frequency/year, typical loss, worst-case loss, participants, assumptions. Expected annual loss is computed automatically.

Capital tab

Enter a period's Business Indicator and average 10-year annual loss. The Business Indicator Component, Loss Component, Internal Loss Multiplier and final Operational Risk Capital are all computed server-side from the Basel SMA formula.

Status flows
draft→workshopped→approved→closed

Model Risk

/model-risk

SR 11-7-style inventory and validation cycle for quantitative and AI/ML models — credit scoring, IFRS 9 ECL, AML monitoring, capital, AI/ML.

How to use it

Add a model (purpose, type, materiality, methodology, owner / developer / vendor, whether it's regulatory-relevant and/or AI/ML) → add Validations underneath it as they occur (type, validator, date, outcome, findings).

Status flow
development→validated→in_production→under_review→retired
Key action

Filter by overdue validation; a summary rolls up counts by status/type and flags overdue validations.

Threat Library

/threat-library

Two reusable reference catalogs — Threats and Vulnerabilities — linked onto risks: a threat exploits a vulnerability to create a risk.

How to use it

Add entries (name, category, description) to either catalog. Linking to a specific risk happens from that risk's Links & Relations tab in the Risk Register, not here. Deleting an entry first shows how many risks currently use it.

ℹ
A static reference catalog — for a live, scanner-fed register see Vulnerabilities instead, a separate, unrelated table.

Risk Quantification

/risk-quantification

FAIR-style Monte Carlo simulation of PKR loss exposure, layered on top of the simpler ALE estimate on the Risk Register.

How to use it

Create a quantification (title, scenario, optionally link a Risk Register entry) → enter Threat Event Frequency and Loss Magnitude as triangular distributions (min / likely / max) → click Run simulation (default 10,000 iterations). P10 / P50 / P90 / mean / max exposure cache on the record.

Status flow
draft→simulated→approved

Running a simulation auto-advances the status.

IT Asset Management

/it-assets

Inventory of supporting/IT assets, judged by replacement cost and availability (RTO/RPO), with criticality that can also be inherited from the information it hosts.

How to use it

Create an asset (media type, cost + currency, availability, environment, hostname / IP / serial / manufacturer) → tag it → set a discovery source (manual, or a connector name) → in the dependency manager, link it to any Information Asset it hosts, stores, processes, transmits, or backs up.

Criticality inheritance

Each asset shows intrinsic_criticality (from its own cost/availability), derived_criticality (the highest business value of everything it hosts), and effective_criticality (the higher of the two). Link assets correctly and the platform surfaces which IT assets are critical because of the data on them — not just their replacement cost.

Connects to

Information Assets (dependency link), Risks, Processes, Legal, Compliance Requirements, Incidents, Exceptions.

Information Assets

/information-assets

Inventory of data/information assets, whose business value is self-assessed by the business owner — not IT or Security.

How to use it

Create an asset (information owner, business value, Confidentiality / Integrity / Availability, handling label — Public / Internal / Confidential / Restricted / PII, data categories, volume) → toggle Self-assessed and record who assessed it and when → in the dependency manager, link it to the IT Asset(s) that host it.

Design intent

Security defines the rating criteria; the business owner does the actual rating via self-assessment — this feeds the IT asset's derived criticality above.

Third Parties

/vendors

The vendor/third-party registry — contacts, criticality, contracts, risk rating, review cycle — referenced by both Outsourcing and Assessments.

How to use it

Create a vendor (category, type, contact, criticality) → Risk & Assessment (risk rating, assessment status, review frequency) → Contracts (value and dates — active contract value auto-sums) → Links (related Risks/Assets).

Status flow
prospective→active→suspended→offboarded
Connects to

Risks (many-to-many), Assets, Outsourcing (optional link back to a vendor), Assessments (via vendor_id).

Outsourcing & Cloud

/outsourcing

The SBP-specific regulatory layer on top of the vendor register — materiality, cloud model, data-offshoring, approval/NOC tracking, exit planning.

How to use it

Create an arrangement (optionally linked to a Vendor) → Materiality & Cloud (material / non-material classification, cloud model, offshoring country) → SBP & Contract (approval required?, status, reference, contract dates) → Exit Plan (plan text + tested?) → periodically add a monitoring Review underneath it.

Status flow
proposed→active→under_review→terminated
Key action

A summary flags contracts expiring within 90 days and exit plans that have never been tested.

Vendor Assessments

/assessments

Send a scored questionnaire to a vendor, capture their answers, auto-score, and track resulting findings.

How to use it

Create an assessment (pick a Questionnaire template, optional Vendor, due date) → answer each question as responses come in (scored option + comment) → Save & submit → raise Findings against weak answers and track them to Close.

Status flow
draft→sent→in_progress→submitted→reviewed

Score % = scored answers ÷ questionnaire max score.

Questionnaires

/questionnaires

The builder for the reusable, weighted-scored questionnaire templates that Assessments sends out.

How to use it

Create a questionnaire → add questions → for each, add answer options with a score (new questions default to Yes=10 / Partial=5 / No=0) → reorder as needed → Save. A live "max score" preview updates as you build.

ℹ
Templates carry no status of their own — the lifecycle lives on the Assessment that uses one.

Compliance

/compliance

Framework and requirement registers — map controls once to satisfy many frameworks, and track compliance gaps.

How to use it

Pick a Framework (or create one, or load a lighter built-in template via Library) → its Requirements populate below → open a requirement's tabs: General, Implementation (status, treatment strategy, efficacy %), Mappings & Crosswalks (link Controls / Risks / Policies / a Legal obligation, and crosswalk to equivalent requirements in other installed frameworks), Audit & Findings (raise / close findings).

Status flow
not_assessed→non_compliant/partially_compliant→compliant
Key action

Gap Analysis — auto-computed per framework, listing uncovered or non-compliant requirements.

Framework Library

/content-library

One-click install of six preloaded, banking-relevant standards.

How to use it

Click Install on a pack: ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A (93 controls), NIST CSF 2.0, SBP ETGRM, PCI DSS v4.0, Basel Operational Risk (7 loss types + 11 PSMOR principles), or SBP/AAOIFI Shariah Governance (12 requirements). This creates a Framework and all its Requirements in Compliance in one step.

ℹ
Re-installing an already-installed pack is blocked (idempotent). Map controls and set up crosswalks back in the Compliance module.

Regulatory Change

/regulatory-change

Track SBP circulars/laws from identification through implementation, distill them into obligations, and manage the recurring returns calendar.

How to use it

Regulatory Changes — log a circular (regulator, circular reference, dates, summary, applicability, impact assessment) → expand it to add Obligations directly underneath. Returns Calendar — recurring submissions with next/last due dates; overdue ones auto-flag.

Status flow
identified→under_assessment→in_implementation→implemented→closed
⚠
Mapped policies/controls on an obligation are free text, not real links — type the name/reference, don't expect a clickable link.

ICFR

/icfr

Run the SBP-mandated annual Internal Control over Financial Reporting cycle.

How to use it

Process & RCM — create a financial process → expand it to add RCM Controls (financial assertion, control type/nature/frequency, design & operating effectiveness) → expand a control to add Tests (sample size, exceptions found, result). Deficiencies — raise one against a process/control whenever a test fails or a control is rated ineffective.

Deficiency severity
deficiency→significant_deficiency→material_weakness
⚠
No dedicated attestation button — use the generic Review & Attestation panel on the control record for management sign-off.

AML / CFT

/aml

Sanctions/PEP/adverse-media screening register, STR/SAR filings to the FMU, and AML/CFT risk assessments.

How to use it

Screening — log a case (subject, screening type, lists checked, match status). STR/SAR — log a suspicious-activity case; the FMU filing deadline auto-calculates from detected date + your configured SLA, and filed date stamps automatically. AML Risk Assessments — periodic inherent/residual risk by customer, product, geography, channel.

SAR status flow
draft→under_review→filed→closed
⚠
This is a register, not a live screening integration — there's no "run screening" button checking an actual sanctions list; results are recorded manually.

Fraud Risk

/fraud

Fraud risk register, fraud case management, and the SBP digital-fraud control checklist — deliberately separate from AML.

How to use it

Fraud Risk Register — log a scheme (channel, business line, inherent/residual scoring, red flags). Fraud Cases — log an incident (amount involved/recovered, customer impact, regulator reporting, root cause). SBP Control Checklist — tick off each requirement as implemented, with an evidence note.

Case status flow
reported→investigating→confirmed→recovered/closed
⚠
Fraud cases are not linked to Basel loss events in Operational Risk — log a shared event in both places if it applies to both.

Control Catalog

/controls

The central, reusable control library referenced by Compliance and Risks.

How to use it

Create a control (objective, description, owner, type, classification) → Cost & Resourcing → Audit & Maintenance (set an audit frequency/success criteria and a separate maintenance frequency) → Links (Policies, Requirements, Risks). Use Record Audit and Record Maintenance periodically — each logs a pass/fail and auto-reschedules the next due date.

Status flow
planned→implemented→operational→retired

Vulnerabilities

/vulnerabilities

Live vulnerability register for scanner findings, and the patch pipeline that remediates them.

How to use it

Vulnerabilities — log a finding (CVE, CVSS score, severity, asset name/IP, source); the remediation deadline auto-sets by severity — critical 7 days, high 30, medium 90, low 180, informational 365 — and flags overdue automatically. Patches — track a patch through its deployment.

Patch status flow
pending→testing→deploying→deployed
⚠
No CSV import and no real link to the Asset inventory — asset name/IP are free text, a "conceptual" link only. Add findings one at a time or via the API for bulk scanner ingestion.

This module has its own free-text impact narrative but is not structurally linked to the dedicated BIA module below — treat them as complementary, not synced.

Business Impact Analysis

/bia

Per-process impact analysis — criticality, RTO/RPO/MTPD, financial and reputational impact — feeding continuity planning.

How to use it

Create an assessment (process, business unit, owner) → Impact & Timing (RTO/RPO/MTPD hours, financial impact at 24h and 1 week, operational / reputational / regulatory / legal impact) → Recovery (minimum resources, recovery strategy, workaround) → add Dependencies underneath (applications, assets, vendors, people, facilities — flag any single point of failure).

Status flow
draft→submitted→approved→retired
Note

Recovery-strategy sign-off happens via the generic Review & Attestation panel, not a dedicated approval button.

Issues & Actions

/issues

The unified CAPA register meant to hold every finding or gap from every other module in one place, through to closure.

How to use it

Create an issue and pick a source type — internal_audit, compliance, rcsa, shariah, assessment, incident, external_inspection, risk_assessment, self_identified, other — plus a free-text source reference pointing back at where it came from. Add CAPA Actions underneath (owner, due date) and log Progress Updates as remediation proceeds.

Status flow
open→in_progress→remediated / closed / risk_accepted
⚠
This is entirely manual today. No other module has a "raise an Issue" button — when you close a finding elsewhere, come here separately to track it in the unified register if you want it here.

Whistleblowing

/whistleblowing

Confidential-disclosure intake and investigation case management.

How to use it

Log a report (category, channel, received date, severity) — toggle Anonymous to suppress reporter name/contact, which auto-generates a tracking code (e.g. WBX-1A2B3C4D) shown in the case header → work it through triage/investigation, logging Case Log entries as you go.

Status flow
received→triage→investigating→substantiated / unsubstantiated / closed
⚠
The tracking code is an internal staff reference only — there's no public page for an anonymous reporter to check status or add a follow-up themselves yet.

Access Reviews

/access-reviews

Periodic user-access certification campaigns.

How to use it

Create a review (system name or linked asset, reviewer, frequency, due date) → add each account under review as an item (username, access held) → for each, click Keep, Revoke, or Reset → once every item has a decision, click Complete review — blocked until all are decided, and auto-schedules the next review from your frequency.

Approvals

/approvals

A generic, genuinely-enforced maker-checker inbox for any request needing independent sign-off.

How to use it

Create a request (title, approver, description, and how many independent checkers are required — 1 for four-eyes, 2 for six-eyes) → checkers Approve or Reject (a reject requires a written reason) → it auto-resolves to approved once enough checkers sign off, or rejected on a single reject.

ℹ
What's real and enforced: the requester can never approve their own request, and each checker can vote only once — checked server-side, not just a UI suggestion.
⚠
Unlike Delegation of Authority, this module's four-eyes logic is actually enforced — but only for requests created here. No other module auto-routes anything through Approvals.

Exceptions

/exceptions

Formal, time-boxed acceptance of a risk, policy, or compliance gap.

How to use it

Create an exception (type, classification, rationale, start/expiry dates, compensating controls) linked to the Risks/Policies/Controls/Requirements/Assets it covers → an approver clicks Approve or Reject → once approved and remediated, click Close.

Status flow
pending→approved / rejected→closed

Projects

/projects

Track remediation projects or initiatives addressing risks, controls, or policies.

How to use it

Create a project (owner, start date, deadline, budget) linked to what it addresses → add Tasks (assignee, due date, % completion — overall progress averages task completion) → log Expenses against the budget (flags over-budget automatically).

Status flow
planned→ongoing→on_hold→completed / cancelled

Activity Log

/audit

The system-wide, read-only, append-only trail of who did what — distinct from the Internal Audit module.

How to use it

View only — every create/update/close/decide/publish/test action across nearly every module writes an entry here automatically. Nothing to configure; use it to answer "who changed this, and when."

Integrations & CCM

/integrations

Register connections to your bank's systems and define automated control tests for Continuous Controls Monitoring.

How to use it

Connectors — register one (AD, O365, SIEM, EDR, CMDB, core banking, cloud — type, endpoint, auth method note, sync frequency); flagged stale automatically after 35+ days without syncing. CCM — define an automated control test (which control it verifies, the pass condition, optionally which connector it uses) → Record a run's result each time it's executed.

⚠
Execution is manual today — no live "test connection" or scheduled auto-run. A recorded run doesn't automatically update the linked Control's effectiveness or create an Issue.

Custom Fields

/custom-fields

Add tenant-specific fields to a record type without code changes.

How to use it

Pick a module — risk, control, asset, vendor, policy, incident, project, goal, exception, processing_activity, continuity_plan, or framework — name the field, pick its type (text/textarea/number/date/select/checkbox), mark required if needed. It then appears automatically in that record type's Custom Fields panel everywhere.

Status Rules

/status-rules

Auto-label records with a colored badge when a field meets a condition.

How to use it

Pick a module (risk, control, incident, vendor, project, policy, asset, goal, or exception), a field and operator (equals / greater-than / contains / overdue / is-true / not-empty…), a comparison value, and a label + color.

ℹ
A labeling engine, not a time-based escalation engine — it doesn't send alerts or reassign anything on its own.

Saved Filters

/filters

Build and reuse named, multi-condition queries over a module.

How to use it

Pick a module, add condition rows (field / operator / value), choose match-all or match-any, mark it Shared if others should see it, then Run it to see matching records.

⚠
A standalone query tool — it doesn't appear as a "load filter" dropdown on each module's own table view.

Import / Export

/data-io

Bulk CSV import/export for 20 supported record types.

How to use it

For any listed resource — Policies, Risks, Controls, Assets, Vendors, Incidents, Exceptions, Legal, Business Units, Processes, Threats, Vulnerabilities catalog, Goals, RoPA, Continuity Plans, Projects, Compliance Requirements, Evidence, Awareness Programs, Access Reviews — click Export, grab a Template, or Import a matching CSV. A per-row result table shows what succeeded or failed; one bad row doesn't block the rest.

Webhooks

/webhooks

Push real-time HTTP notifications to an external system whenever records change.

How to use it

Create a webhook (name, payload URL, events — a comma list like risk,incident,approval, or * for everything, optional signing secret) → Test it → Enable it. Check the Log for recent deliveries.

⚠
Delivery is single-shot, best-effort — no automatic retry if the receiving end is briefly down.

SSO

/sso-settings

Configure OIDC/OAuth2 sign-in with your bank's identity provider.

How to use it

Toggle SSO enabled, enter your IdP's Client ID/Secret and authorize/token/userinfo URLs, set the email/name claim names, toggle JIT provisioning and pick the default role new SSO users get, and optionally restrict to specific email domains.

ℹ
LDAP/Active Directory is configured separately, on the Settings page below.

Settings

/settings

The general admin hub — organization info, system health, personal security, and LDAP.

What's here

Organization/role summary, a "Send test email" button, read-only system health/version/license info, personal MFA and password change, the LDAP/Active Directory configuration card (host, bind DN, base DN, user filter, default role for JIT users), and an Administration hub linking out to Users & Roles, SSO, Webhooks, Custom Fields, Status Rules, Saved Filters, Import/Export, and the Activity Log.

End-to-end workflows

Worked examples of how modules chain together in practice.

A · Taking a risk from identification to closure

Threat Library → Risk Register → assess inherent likelihood × impact → link a Control as treatment, collect Evidence against it → optional Risk Quantification Monte Carlo → review on a set frequency → close or formally accept.

B · Responding to a new SBP circular

AI Assist extracts obligations → Regulatory Change record + obligations → update the relevant Compliance framework requirements (install a new pack from the Framework Library first if needed) → update the affected Policy → if there's a gap, manually raise an Issue (source_type = regulatory_change) → add any recurring submission to the Returns Calendar.

C · Onboarding and assessing a vendor

Vendors (status: prospective) → Questionnaires (build or reuse a template) → Assessments (send it, capture answers, review findings) → if SBP-material or cloud, add an Outsourcing record linked to the same vendor → link the vendor to related Risk Register entries → move to active with a recurring review frequency.

D · Handling a security incident

Security Operations — log, work the NIST response stages to closure → if reportable, generate regulatory reports and mark submitted once filed → manually create an Issue (source_type = incident) if root-cause remediation needs longer tracking → link affected Controls/Assets/Vendors/Risks for context.

E · Running the annual ICFR cycle

ICFR — build the process universe → add RCM controls with financial assertions → run tests each cycle → a failed test or ineffective control raises a Deficiency, classified by severity, and remediated → sign off via the Review & Attestation panel on each control.

F · An internal audit engagement

Internal Audit — maintain the risk-based Audit Universe → create an Engagement against a unit → record Procedures as fieldwork proceeds → raise Findings → track them on the Findings follow-up tab until closed → optionally mirror a significant finding into Issues & Actions manually.

G · Asset criticality inheritance

Information Assets — business owner self-assesses business value + CIA → link it to the IT Asset(s) that host it → the IT asset's derived_criticality inherits the highest business value hosted, and effective_criticality should drive incident-response prioritization and vulnerability remediation urgency.

H · Operational-risk loss to Basel capital

Operational Risk — log a loss event with its Basel type → over time, Scenario Analysis workshops future scenarios using observed frequency/severity as a starting point → the Capital tab computes Basel III SMA operational risk capital from your Business Indicator and average annual losses.

I · A whistleblowing case

Whistleblowing — intake, toggle Anonymous if needed → triage → investigate, logging Case Log entries → resolve to substantiated / unsubstantiated / closed.

No public follow-up portal exists yet for the reporter — see Known gaps.

J · Maker-checker, today

Delegation of Authority documents who should approve what — a reference matrix, not enforced. For an approval you need enforced today, create the request directly in Approvals, set the required number of independent checkers, and reference the record it concerns in the description.

Roles & permissions

Every permission is a resource:action code — 93 total across every module. Most follow simple read/write; a few sensitive actions get their own verb (e.g. risk:accept, exception:approve) so "can edit" and "can approve" can be different people.

Built-in roleTypical use
AdminFull access, including user/role management.
Risk ManagerRisk, controls, assets, incidents, vendors, BCP, projects, operational risk, fraud, scenario analysis, vulnerabilities, model risk, outsourcing, quantitative risk, AI Assist.
Risk ApproverRead-only across risk/controls/assets, plus approve actions only (risk acceptance, exceptions, approvals) — a pure "checker" role.
Compliance ManagerFrameworks, policies, privacy, awareness, Shariah, AML, issues, regulatory change, ICFR, declarations, whistleblowing, governance, ESG, DoA, data protection.
AuditorRead-only across the entire platform, plus full read/write on Internal Audit — preserving independence.
ViewerRead-only everywhere.

Admins can also build custom roles by hand-picking any combination of the 93 permissions under Users & Roles. Assignment is per-user — there's no per-record or per-business-unit scoping.

ℹ
When the platform is upgraded with new modules — and therefore new permission codes — every built-in role is automatically topped up with the codes it's entitled to, the next time the backend restarts. This is additive only: it never removes a grant, and never touches custom roles or a user's individual assignments. In practice, you won't hit an unexpected 403 on a newly-added module after an upgrade without having to manually re-grant anything.

Known gaps — things that look automatic but aren't yet

Documented so you plan your bank's process around what's actually enforced, not what's merely configured.

  • ⚠
    Delegation of Authority / dual-control rules are a registry, not an enforcement engine. Defining a limit or a maker-checker rule doesn't gate any write anywhere else yet.
  • ⚠
    Approvals isn't auto-triggered by other modules. You create the request yourself and reference the source record manually; the four-eyes logic once a request exists is genuinely enforced.
  • ⚠
    Issues & Actions is entirely manually populated. No other module has a "raise an Issue" button.
  • ⚠
    Business Units are not a security boundary. Only whole-organization isolation is enforced at the database level.
  • ⚠
    Whistleblowing's tracking code is internal-only. No public self-service status check or follow-up page yet.
  • ⚠
    Fraud Cases and Operational Risk Loss Events are separate, unlinked registers — log a shared event in both if it applies to both.
  • ⚠
    Integrations/CCM runs are manually recorded, not live or automatic; a run doesn't update a Control's effectiveness or auto-create an Issue.
  • ⚠
    Webhook delivery has no retry — a failed POST is logged, not retried.
  • ⚠
    Saved Filters run from their own page, not as a live filter dropdown on each module's table.
  • ⚠
    Reporting is on-demand, not scheduled. Four PDF exports exist — Risk Register, Executive Summary, Audit Engagement Report, Shariah Review Report — no "email me this every Monday."
  • ⚠
    Record-level immutability for loss events, SAR, and breach records — a regulator expectation — is not yet built.
  • ⚠
    New organization/tenant self-service creation has no frontend page — it's an operator-run backend step today.
